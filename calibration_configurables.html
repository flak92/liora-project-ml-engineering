Golden Calibration · methodology branch

Calibration configurables

Every tunable number in the ladder, as a range — with where it is calibrated, what it depends on, and which knob to widen when a step comes up empty. The distinction that runs through the whole page: a ADMISSIBLE number is a hypothesis you may widen; a FROZEN number is the standard of proof and may not be tuned away.

Structural

frozen @ Rung 0 — interval, label horizon, barriers, costs, embargo, the OOS ban. One value; moving it changes the problem.

Calibratable

data-fitted range — gamma, min_child_weight, θ-grid, block length, permutation count, HPO budget. Never one absolute number across assets.

Searched

the objects — features, families, interactions, subsets. What the ladder looks for, gated at each rung.

Methodology-governing

proof standard — viability floor, rotations, α, early-stop. Frozen so a null result cannot be tuned away.

Dependency order & the widen loop

Inside one outer fold the series calibrate in order — the feature set feeds HPO, HPO freezes, selection runs at frozen params, and the operating point θ is chosen last. A stuck rung never auto-widens: it stops and asks for the next hand-authored contract version.

Proof-standard gates

FROZEN — the numbers that decide how much proof is enough. Not ranges, not widenable. The contract kernel forbids any ladder patch from touching them; changing one is a new science, done by hand.

Widen protocol

Which knob when a rung yields nothing. Only NEEDS_CONTRACT widens, and only the ADMISSIBLE space — widen a range where the object is a hypothesis, never where it is the standard of proof.

Rung yields nothingStateWiden-target (next contract version)
1 · no viable modelNEEDS_CONTRACTmodel_space ranges / hpo_trials — never the viability floor
3 · no marginal candidateRESOLVED_EMPTYfeature families / candidates, or the θ-grid
4 · no confirmed featureRESOLVED_EMPTYθ-grid / arms
5 · no stable survivorRESOLVED_EMPTY— the null is the proof standard; a negative is the result
6 · retained = 0RESOLVED— a valid, complete outcome
